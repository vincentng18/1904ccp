2. PRODUCT DESIGN





RMB Memory Package (1/12/2021)


From last week design, this week I made a few developments. I showed some designers my memory pack which received feedback of being to complicated with colors. Users might rather care about happiness and sadness only than the whole 8 emotions. Therefore, I simplifed the pill color to only 2 colors: yellow and purple.

This choice reduced considerable size of the memory pack, to 19 cm x 11cm, bigger than the size of a calculator a little bit, almost equal to a pocket notebook.

Bespoke Memory Package: Your Memory Pattern On Demand - Generative Art (2/12/2021)


After my presumed complete product format, there was a next step quite important for me: the “bespoke” characteristic of the brand. Due to the personal memory, the product must be customized with personal pattern/graphic/images of users. This part led me to a field called Generative Art - a process of algorithmically generating new ideas, forms, shapes, colors or patterns.

This idea was inspired by the project of The Lab Saigon for Vietcetera Podcast︎︎︎: they designed a software that takes audio data and generates a spectrogram artwork.


The Lab Saigon, Vietcetera Podcast Generative Spectrogram, 2021.

Therefore, I did some research on simple Generative Art. Spectrogram is a type of Generative Art for sound and audio, while my project mostly focuses on images. I used p5.js - a JavaScript library with the goal of making coding accessible to artists, designers, educators, and beginners.

I followed a tutorial guiding creating generative art using image data, which aims to a pictorial as a result. The coding process was really bloody and painful, my laptop got lag several times and one missing character in the code line can lead to error. 

The image data would be distorted by pixel and creating blurry, swirling movement quite similar to glass and oil painting.

RMB Memory Package (3/12/2021)


The memory pack design so far only has the backing. Actually without the lid, it would still look alright as ordinary tablet/pill packs. Adding a lid might help securing the product more effectively? I still didn’t decide.

At the same time I was sketching and considering the secondary packaging as well. At first, I was inspired by the full-package solution of Ritual - a company providing women supplements - as they put everything into a box︎︎︎: the pill bottle with collateral design. So I was intending to make a box like that. But I soon got hit by its shortcomings: (1) have to make more items to put into the box (booklet, manual, card, etc.) (2) unsuitable and ineffective with the number of memory (ranging from day to month to year). This idea not only put more stress on me about designing more (unnecessary) items, struggling with complicated packaging design, but also add unconvenience on customer with a cumbersome package.


A very complicated packaging idea I got at first. I consulted the video packaging dieline from Volpe & Associates︎︎︎. But I quickly met the disadvantages of this idea - unnecessary complicating. I didn’t even know how should I fold it.


The Tablet Facts was inspired by the Drug Facts and Supplement Facts label, for important information and indication of the memory pack. At first I was designing it as a card to stick into the package. But it could easily get lost, while they are essential content, should be  inseparable with the pill pack (I was worried a lot for the customer since this is a product easy to missuse, overuse, or even abused, based on the trust I have with myself). 

Meanwhile, I was testing the pill pack format. Then I came up with the idea: Why don’t I put the Tablet Facts onto the lid of the pill pack/box? They would be inseparable, constantly remind the user of how should they use this product. 


RMB Memory Package - Official Version 1.


I considered this version got a much much closer step to the complete version. 

In this version, I put every Tablet Facts onto the back side of the lid. I used the blank space left for personal information: customer information, this memory name, timeline, reason they chose to extract these memories. 

For example memory used for (fictional) producing the very first packages, I used 3 people in total: 

(1) Myself (Ms. Butterfly Pea): 3-or-4-month memory (cause these memory are very accurate and correct).
(2) My friend Hieu’s memory 
(3) My friend designer Ine’s memory of her joyful trip.

Therefore, I can experiment on both sad and happy memories, as well as different timeline.

RMB Memory Package - 3D Render (11/6/2021)


This stage I worked with a 3D designer to make the product come to life. In the 1st attempt we mostly worked on how the box functions: the user would open the box, choose the memories they want to remember by date, peel off the transparent seal, then take the pills they wish to have.

The outside of the box was coated with tin so it’s a little bit shiny. To match with the distorting texture of the picture, the surface of the box is slightly rough. Every text on the outside of the box was embossed to slightly pop up.

In the Tablet Facts inside, the text “Tablet Facts” was slightly embossed, while the picture area was debossed with the text on top of it embossed. The rest of the lid was printed in normal technique.

The text “RMB”, “You are responsible for your own memory. Consume wisely”, the connect line, the color block of Happy and Sad were all embossed to visibly appear.

The pill pockets have transparent seal with date on top of it to help user identify which day of that memory.
