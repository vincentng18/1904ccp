Brand & Identity Development/
Mockups


Since my lecturer was sick on the fixed tutorial day, I continued to work on my own without having feedback to put on the top of the weekly page. Anyway, I was still excited to develop my brand identity. This process was getting into more interesting stages.



Why can my Memory Package be a product and also not?

This is to make clear about my confusing final “product”.

A real-product requires a lot of stages in a process, to create a functional product to meet the (realistic) need of customer. I can say that a real-life product is not totally a surprise, since the producers have to work for a while to make sure people know about the intention, give out their need about that product.

Design Fiction’s prototype doesn’t work that way. It’s a prop, an object for exhibition purpose, to be viewed, to trigger people asking questions and discussion. It makes people wonder why do it exist. 

I don’t prove this for skipping the product research/data gather stage. But we should understand the making of a functional object is similar to making a piece of art. The major difference here is the adjective “functional”. The prototype should look “real” and “functional”, to dismiss the boundary between fiction and reality, present and (near) future.

POSTER SERIES

There were some poster layout experiments I’ve done throughout the week. Since my purpose was to make them minimal and cinematic, I focused more on the typographic layout and photography treatment, in order to make the best use of lenticular effect. My struggle was mostly hierarchy (as week 5’s experiment showed my scattering layout and week contrast). In week 6’s experiment, I finally can distribute the context “layers” from big to small component: the very big title (RMB logo), medium display text (the slogan) and small text (contact info and small bodytext). Once the layout is complete, I would adjust the image treatment and color application.

The 3rd experiment was more like the picture got on top of the content, rather than the content creating a frame. I was sitting on the fences for a while because of this. The 2nd layout divided the layout clearly between image and context, when the 3rd got the layout confusing since there were already some fixed elements such as the play button, time counting and the SLP (super long play). 
I was struggle for a few days cause the key visual of the brand seemed not to be strong enough. I got the editorial layout but it didn’t strike as much as I thought. The color palette was not made the best use of so the vibe lacked of something. I also felt doubtful with the image elements if they should be there. So I turned to the VHS element while still trying to maintain the frame layout and made a few try with the Set of Rule. 
With the gradient option for color palette, it looked... quite nice? I used only three color for the gradient mesh, since I thought about hologram effect as well. Maybe motion-blurred image wouldn’t look as suggestive and emotional as the the gradient, looked better than the flat. I was just worried if the video tape frame layout would be dominated by the color. And I might have to extend the color palette if this would be the option.

MOCKUP

The difficulty in this stage was the material manipulation. You cannot just download a mockup then put your image on. Instead of, there must be initiated plan that which element on the design would be embossed, die cut, foiled, so on. The texture is important the same. Just don’t make the mockup image look flat and cheap.

My mockup was not for design showing off but speculating how the design would be in real life. That’s why I started this process right after the finish of basic brand identity and during the application design.

BUSINESS CARD

It was kinda soon to create mockup, business card mockup in particular. But I couldn’t help wondering and testing the printing technique that I plan. My intention was to emboss the “Play” and time button on the back side of the business card. But material manipulation was indeed complicated 😭️.
The 1st attempt looked quite wrong cause I used some grain-textured effect for the business card. With normal card, this would make the card thicker and condensed. However, I already have some lenticular card at home and the apprerance looked totally different. It constantly has serrations (in vertical lines) and looks shiny rather than matted.

ENVELOPE

The stage of business card and envelope and cover letter was alongside the key visual testing. Although I had multipule option with them, I still found it not convincing to myself. They were not striking and fictional enough.

Some close-up details of testing the material (paper texture and debossed logo). But the mood was not quite right.
Then I tried the gradient color combining with foil printing. The problem is, if every background are black (to highlight the gradient area), we would not be able to write anything onto the envelope...

The slogan was debossed onto the hologram flap.

This choice of color might make me work on the basic identity once again... ︎
