WEEK 7+8


Brand & Identity Development/Mockups/ Proposal Update


In these two week I was struggling with my brand & identity, as well as re-aproaching the project.








LECTURER’S FEEDBACK ON WEEK 5 AND 6

He was worried about me 😰️ 
  • The art direction and moodboard should be my pictures. Or credited carefully. Even the pictures I used for brand introduction shouldn’t be stock photo since RMB is a very unique brand.
  • The work I was doing was not the most important part of the project.
  • The deliverables should emphasize the service aspect of this brand - customer experience in space, promotional/advertising items in space, how they interact with my service (hand in card, get consulting). Imagine you’re a part of a film setting, how should my deliverables do their work and do the storytelling?




MY AFTERTHOUGHT

Although it was still very sad to hear the feedback... I still got the point though. It’s not how the design looks but performs. Rather than making good design, I should create “story” of how users use my service and interact with the brand’s items. In my proposal file, I did lack of the brand message and service demonstration. 

Just don’t think about the design. Think about the place and situation where they are used and applied. I will work on this once again.

The difficulty of this project is about the product and 3D stuff, cause 3D is not my major area. If I ask a 3D designer to collab with me, this might be solved. However it’s not quite right to let somebody do a big part of my project, so I think about some alternative solution for this. About the “in-space” narrative of the brand, I can do manipulation from real-life photographs rather than building up a whole 3D scene.

I actually did think about this. Because my brand is fictional and near-future, I couldn’t mockup my promotional items in normal scene. Taking photographs of Hanoi and manipulating into near-future cyberpunk scene was an idea I came up with. So I think I should focus on this part from now. 

DELIVERABLES

From the lecturer’s feedback, I tried to re-approach my project from other perspective: customer first, not the design. It’s not like the branding could be done sketchy. I still went on with the brand design, but now I have to think about the scenes I need for this fictional brand.

So the deliverable will consist of: ~20 images, brand design and product design.

REFERENCES

The lecturer mentioned a valuable suggestion: film props - which is also discussed in my thesis︎︎︎ about Design Fiction about how the diegetic prototype works. From the references I consult from the movie Eternal Sunshine of The Spotless Mind and the Korean music video What Do I Call You (Taeyeon - SM Entertainment) which referenced the movie as well, I noted down some important scenarios:

1. The company office.
2. Pre-procedure interviews.
3. The “mapping” of emotional cores in the brain based on objects and memories.
4. The memory erasure process.

Both of the movie and the music video lacks of urban promotion - advertisement in the city. From the research, I can list the important scenarios (in space) for my “fiction”:

1. The company office - How do the brand look like?
2. Urban promotion - How do that world look like?
3. The user using the service and product - How do people behave in that world?

︎︎︎ SO MY PROJECT PLAN HAS 4 MAIN TASKS

1. The Branding

2. The company office

This part I would work with an interior designer to sketch out the space and suitable scene for mockups. There are some necessary set for the RMB company:
 
- Entrance & Building.
- Waiting room.
- Consulting room.

3. Urban Promotion

My idea for this scenario is taking pictures of Hanoi and manipulate them into cyberpunk/sci-fi style. My inspiration was from a 3D project named Robotic Soul︎︎︎. Also, my project was propositioned in a near-future vision where human race gradually got caught up in the "hamster wheel of life", which leads to the increasing of mental issues. So in my imagination, Hanoi in that era would be a busy, highly technology city with a dark vibe of dystopia.

4. The Product

This part I would work with an 3D designer to create the Memory Package, from the sketching to 3D render.


︎ Dividing the project into 4 main tasks help me overview the project better (and less panic because of so many work I have to do but didn’t know where to begin). I can also work on all 4 of this gradually each week.

︎ BRANDING -
POSTER & KEY VISUALS

From the lecturer’s feedback, my project despite of being about memory, has no images. Therefore, I tried on another idea: blur images. Since memory is incorrect recall, blurry images might be a typical trait. If I turn to this idea, the key visual must be worked once again (actually at the moment it already did). The color palette would not be the point (cause multiple pictures have a wide range of color), there might be only black and white.

With the first try, I used my own film photographs (which are, obviously, significant memories) as the stock. However, I received the feedback from a friend that these vintage pictures didn’t give enthusiasm for the customer to use the service. Like, if you design the brand for an insurance company and use bright, lively pictures, customer will trust cause those images are the future they wish to be. My pictures can be nostalgic and vacanny at the same time. So I still consider.

Maybe if I can create my own (blur) images by 3D this would be more manageable and authentic? Also I still want to make use of my branding color palette.
