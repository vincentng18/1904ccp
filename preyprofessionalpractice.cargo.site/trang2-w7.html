Concept Development



GLENN’S FEEDBACK
  • The concept development improves a lot from the last week. Now you have plenty of small ideas to choose in particular.
  • The prey’s behaviour linked to design principles is a good idea alone. You can make your project showing off your design principle/graphic design specialist skill. When you make the viewer play with the design element, the prey has become the leading predator. An example is the Made You Look︎︎︎ book cover of Stefan Sagmeister.
  • Mind your visual styling.
  • The self-making kit can become a hunting kit (like Vampire Hunting Kit) with each item having different function. The multifunctioning binder is a good choice for this.
  • Old English hunting lodge: your project might have this vintage feeling, but can be contemporary too.
MY THOUGHTS
I got excited the most with the idea of hunting binder kit. The only problem is that stuff is really hard to custom. If I want to make one on my own, I should order it (from abroad).

Update: I already ordered the binder (A4 size) for customization 😇️
My plan with the binder is I would heat print on the cover to make my own binder cover one. Then I made promotional items to put inside the binder as a set.

THE SKETCHES

I listed out as many hunting items as I could think of, so the design the self-making kit items can take form of them.
Since the portfolio is the biggest part of the kit - it means the portfolio contains mostly every key visual and graphic element, I started with it first. I sketched out my idea for the (numbered) pages: 
(1) Cover.
(2) Blank page.
(3) Introduction.
(4-5) Prey’s profile + CV.
(6-7) Table of content - Hunting map.

Each section of the portfolio (branding, editorial design, typography, personal projects) - hunting area would have its special cover - a type of prey. I chose some prey relating to each anti-predation behaviour:
(1) Warding Of Attack - Moth - Branding.
(2) Escaping - Rabbit - Editorial Design.
(3) Chemical Defence (Fight Back) - Poisonous Frog - Typography.
(4) Prey-Predator Reversal - Wolf - Personal Projects.

In the sketches, I tried from illustration style to graphic style (more graphic, less complicated).
During my research of hunting documents, I found some important reference, which were:
(1) Field Guide - a book for the identification of birds, flowers, minerals, or other things in their natural environment: this influenced my hunting kit/portfolio and hinted a lot of useful format.
(2) Vintage Advertisement: this relates to some weapon and hunting law announcement in 30s-60s. It has some stuff to do with layout design and typography of this hunting kit.
